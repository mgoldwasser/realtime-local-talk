# Q1 2026 — Fixed Income Duration Positioning

**Author:** TAA Research Desk
**Date:** 2026-02-04

## Summary

We are extending portfolio duration from **5.4 to 6.1 years**, a modest
overweight against the policy benchmark of 5.7. The move is funded by trimming
cash and short-duration corporates. The thesis: real yields have peaked, and
the FOMC is more likely to cut twice than once in 2026.

## Positioning

- Increase TLT exposure by **+150 bps** versus policy.
- Reduce SHV (1-3 month T-bills) by **-100 bps**.
- Reduce LQD (IG corporate, ~9.5y duration) by **-50 bps** — we like the
  duration but not the spread compensation here.

## Rationale

- The Fed's December dot plot showed median 2026 funds rate at 3.625% versus
  market pricing of 3.875%. We see the market eventually converging to the
  dots.
- Real yields on 10-year TIPS have fallen 35 bps from October peak; the term
  premium component looks fully priced.
- A small overweight in duration helps the portfolio's diversification
  benefit in a soft-landing or mild-recession scenario.

## Risks

The main risk is a re-acceleration of services inflation, which would force
the Fed to hold longer than the market expects. We monitor super-core CPI,
PCE services ex-shelter, and the Atlanta Fed wage growth tracker.

## Hedges

We retain a small position in 5-year payer swaptions, struck 50 bps out of
the money, as a hedge against the inflation re-acceleration tail.
