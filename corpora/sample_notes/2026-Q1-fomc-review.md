# January 2026 FOMC Meeting Review

**Author:** Macro Research
**Date:** 2026-01-30

## Decision

The FOMC held the target range at **4.25-4.50%**, as widely expected. The
statement removed the reference to "additional firming," signaling that the
hiking cycle is over. The dot plot was not updated at this meeting.

## Press conference highlights

Chair Powell described policy as "modestly restrictive" and emphasized the
committee's data dependence. He pushed back against a March cut but
acknowledged that the bar for further hikes is high.

Notable quote: "We need to see a continuation of the disinflation process
before we are confident that inflation is moving sustainably to 2%."

## Market reaction

- 2-year Treasury: -8 bps to 4.12%
- 10-year Treasury: -4 bps to 4.18%
- DXY: -0.35%
- S&P 500: +0.65% on the dovish lean
- Fed funds futures: implied probability of a March cut moved from 41% to 28%

## TAA implications

This meeting validates our duration overweight (see the duration note from
February). The dovish lean supports our recently raised EM equities view
through the dollar channel. We make no further changes at this meeting and
will reassess after the February jobs print and CPI.
