# Q1 2026 — Credit Outlook (IG and HY)

**Author:** Credit Research, with TAA overlay
**Date:** 2026-02-11

## Summary

We are **neutral on investment grade** and moving from neutral to **modest
underweight high yield**. Spreads have compressed to levels that do not
adequately compensate for default risk into a slowing growth backdrop.

## Spread levels

- IG OAS (Bloomberg US Corp Index): 92 bps, 24th percentile since 2010.
- HY OAS (Bloomberg US Corporate HY): 308 bps, 18th percentile since 2010.
- HY spread per turn of leverage: lowest since 2017.

These levels imply complacency. We do not see a triggering event but the
risk-reward of being long HY is asymmetric here.

## Positioning

- HY: **-75 bps** versus policy via HYG trim. Reallocate into intermediate
  Treasuries.
- IG: hold policy weight via LQD; modest curve preference for 7-10y.
- Loans: small overweight (+25 bps) — floating rate buffers against any
  yield curve re-steepening.

## Sector views

Prefer financials over industrials in IG. In HY, avoid leveraged real
estate (CRE refinancing wall in 2026/27) and consumer discretionary.
Energy HY looks fair.

## Risks

Tighter labor market keeps default rates suppressed, in which case the
underweight underperforms. We will re-evaluate if HY OAS widens beyond
375 bps or compresses below 280 bps.
