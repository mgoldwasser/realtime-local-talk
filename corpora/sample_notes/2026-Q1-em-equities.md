# Q1 2026 — Emerging Markets Equity Outlook

**Author:** TAA Research Desk
**Date:** 2026-01-22

## Summary

We are moving from neutral to **modestly overweight** EM equities for Q1 2026,
funded by a corresponding underweight in US large-cap growth. The thesis rests
on three signals: a peaking US dollar, easing China policy bias, and a widening
EM-vs-DM earnings revision gap.

## Positioning

Recommended tilt: **+100 bps** above policy benchmark on broad EM (EEM/VWO
basket); within EM, prefer Korea and Brazil over India on valuation; underweight
China A-shares versus offshore China.

Risk budget contribution: ~6.4% of total active risk, well within the 12%
guideline. Tracking error contribution is ~22 bps annualized.

## Key drivers

- **Dollar:** DXY has rolled over from the November peak; carry-adjusted EM FX
  shows positive momentum in Brazil real and South African rand.
- **China policy:** the December stimulus package was larger than consensus
  expected; PBoC RRR cut signaled in the most recent press conference.
- **Earnings:** MSCI EM 12-month forward EPS revisions are +1.4% vs MSCI USA
  at -0.6%; the gap is the widest since Q2 2024.

## Risks

A re-acceleration in US growth and rising real yields would reverse the dollar
thesis quickly. We monitor 10-year breakevens and the OIS-implied path for the
next two FOMC meetings. A China trade tariff shock remains a tail risk.

## Implementation

Express through EEM and VWO at a 60/40 weight; trim QQQ and IWM as the funding
source. Expected implementation cost: ~2.1 bps. Review at the February TAA
committee.
